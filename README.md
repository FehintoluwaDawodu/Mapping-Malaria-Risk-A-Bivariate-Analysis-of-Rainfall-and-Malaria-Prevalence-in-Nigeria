# Mapping-Malaria-Risk-A-Bivariate-Analysis-of-Rainfall-and-Malaria-Prevalence-in-Nigeria
 

## Overview
- Malaria remains a public health concern in Nigeria.
- Environmental factors such as rainfall can influence mosquito breeding and disease transmission.
- Understanding spatial patterns helps improve prevention and control strategies.

## Data and Methodology
- Malaria prevalence data
- Rainfall data
- Analytical method: Bivariate thematic mapping
- QGIS

![MAP](https://github.com/FehintoluwaDawodu/Mapping-Malaria-Risk-A-Bivariate-Analysis-of-Rainfall-and-Malaria-Prevalence-in-Nigeria/blob/main/Relationship%20between%20Malaria%20and%20Rainfall.png)


## Findings
- Rainfall shows a partial relationship with malaria prevalence.
- Only 12 out of 37 states show similar rainfall and malaria proportions.
- Some states, such as Kebbi State, show different patterns, suggesting additional influencing factors

## Conclusion
- Rainfall alone does not fully explain malaria prevalence across Nigeria.
- A multifactorial approach is necessary to understand malaria risk.
